Traditional Food from Northeast of Brazil - June Festival

Junine party or June festival is a huge celebration that happens in June at the Northeast of Brazil. This party started as a way to celebrate the grain harvest, like corn, and it became popular. That is one of the reason why corn is the base for many Brazilian' Northeast traditional food.

Besides the classic boiled corn, there are many other recipes prepared with corn, like canjica (sweet corn pudding), pamonha (corn-paste), manguzá, corn cake, cuscuz, popcorn, and so on. Some of this dishes are made specially during June Festival, but others are made the whole year.

It is also common to have others traditional dishes during June celebrations. It does not have corn as a base, but it also reminds the harvest period. Some of this dishes are cassava cake, sweet potato cake, sweet rice, tapioca, pé de moleque, and cocada.

This traditional foods has a special space in my heart, and that is why I decided to share some of this delicious and flavory recipes here. I hope you all enjoy it.
